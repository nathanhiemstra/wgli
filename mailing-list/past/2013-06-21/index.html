Recovery Story
"It Was Kind of Accidental"

I was actually looking for an AA meeting and ended up in an ACA meeting.  I peeked into the room and noticed that the books were red instead of blue. The people saw me peeking in so they invited me to come in. In AA, I learned how to put the “plug in the jug,” but ACA helped in my search for self-discovery and understanding why I do what I do.  I was more of a reactor than an actor.  I reacted a lot in anger whenever I was hurt.  The process of being open and willing to trust the process helped me to get from reacting to acting.  I was angry because I was raised by a single parent and my grandparents.  What is normal?  Normal for me was growing up without a father.  I had abandonment issues and grew up hating my father.  I was always told I looked just like him. I felt that my grandmother could be mean, and there were times when my uncles and mom were physically abusive.  Then my mom got remarried, and I actually felt like excess baggage.  I told her that he would treat his children better than he treated me, and I was right – he did.  I always felt like I was in but out with my family.  

It’s interesting how what you grow up with directly relates to how you think about yourself.   I finally feel like I’m coming into a complete person without having to second guess myself.  This never would’ve happened without involvement in 12 step groups, particularly ACA.  There are just so many things that I’ve learned.  So, my journey and subsequent progressive recovery due to ACA all happened by accident.  I didn’t get the meeting that I wanted, but I did get the meeting that I needed.   And I’ve been coming back ever since.

Featured Meeting

Wednesday 7pm Red Text Study - North Side Alano Club, Chicago

This meeting was designed to help us procrastinators get through the Red Text by reading it together, and to give us more insight through sharing after each reading.

Our format: We read for 13 minutes, journal or meditate for seven minutes, then share. The book is so rich with fantastic information that sharing and hearing the responses of others has been an integral part of understanding the messages and bringing inspiration.

The meeting usually has 5-10 participants with a very consistent group of core attendees. We got through the book in a year and nine months and are now going through it for a second time.

For more information, call Nate 773-209-6300.

Global ACA News
2013 ACA World Service Annual Business Conference (ABC) held in Birkerod, Denmark

This ABC was the first ever held outside of the U.S., and it was a very unifying experience.  There were 60 participants with 34 voting delegates.  Countries represented besides the U.S. and Denmark were Finland, Germany, Hungary, Italy, Latvia, Norway, Poland, Russia, Spain, Sweden, and the U.K.

There were 2 Ballot items from our West Great Lakes Intergroup that were up for discussion and voting, and the results of the voting are:

  • Proposal to create a 12 Tradition pamphlet:  
    This was not approved.  
    We might want to consider proposing again next year with different criteria about what it should contain.
  • Proposal to create information about different ways to do the Workbook and write questions for some of the Steps that don’t have them:  
    This was approved.  
    Mary Jo, Intergroup Chair, will be working with volunteers from Finland on this project, but anyone else is welcome to contribute.  She has already received input from some Nashville meetings.
