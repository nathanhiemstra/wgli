Mar 22, 2013 - Mar 24, 2013 

The workshop took place at the Transformations Spirituality Center in Kalamazoo, Michigan. It was a nice group of around 60 dedicated ACAs who had been attending this workshop for years. There were two speakers:

Recovery Story
“An Ounce of Inspiration”

I went to my first ACA meeting because I was desperate. I was desperate for help, I was desperate for hope and I was desperate for others who could identify with me. At that first meeting I found people who shared similar stories and others who could relate to my story.

But I left feeling both thankful and disheartened. I was thankful that I had found a recovery meeting with like-minded people, but in the same breath I was disheartened that I had “just” found a recovery meeting. Where had these meetings and these people been all of my life. Why didn't someone tell me they existed. I then realized that I could choose to focus on my disappointments or my newfound blessings. Thankfully, because I kept remembering what was said at the end, “Keep coming back,” that’s exactly what I did.

Now, years later, when the newcomer comes into the meeting with the thoughts and feelings that I once had, when the newcomer doesn’t believe that there is hope and that their life can get better - yet they look to me and others in the program for encouragement, I tell them the same thing that I was told years ago: “If you want what we’ve got, keep coming back!”

Featured Meeting
Hyde Park (Saturday Morning) — Chicago, IL

The Hyde Park meeting is a closed ACA meeting that meets at the church of St. Paul the Redeemer located at 4945 S. Dorchester Avenue. The meeting begins at 9:00 a.m. and lasts for approximately one and a half hours. There is plenty of free parking. The group members rotate chairing the meeting and share the other responsibilities of the meeting.

The meeting format is as follows for each month: The first Saturday the group studies steps 1-3. The second and third Saturdays focus on steps 4-12 in their respective order using the workbook until completion. After completion, the process begins again. The last Saturday of the month is reserved for a Tradition.

Group members experience fellowship after the meeting, sometimes with casual conversation and other times by going out for a bite to eat or for a walk in the park across the street.

This meeting is small, ranging from three to seven members. We welcome the support of others. The contact person for this meeting is Fabian, who can be reached at 773-744-8589.

Annual Business Conference details
ABC Ballots: Your Vote Counts!!

In the 4th Quarter of every year, any Meeting or Intergroup from around the globe is able to propose ballot items that ask the ACA Fellowship to consider their ideas. In February of each year, all ballot items are collated and sent to Meetings and Intergroups asking them to vote on whether they believe each item should be able to be discussed at the ABC.

It is important that all groups vote because these issues may ultimately affect your particular group and/or ACA as a whole. Please get involved and ask your meeting to submit their vote. NOTE: It’s also possible to vote on only some of the issues if your meeting does not want to tackle all of them.

It is important that all meetings vote for a couple of reasons:

First, these issues may affect your particular group and/or ACA as a whole.

Secondly, only ballot items that receive a 'yes' vote by 2/3 of the meetings that vote will be discussed at the Annual Business Conference.
